Things good to know about spacegroups

  • There are 230 distinct space group types in 3D. They are frequently referred to by a the sequence number in which they appear in the International Tables for Crystallography (IT), 1−230.
  • Taking into account options for axis ordering and origin choice gives alternative ways to represent these, called "settings". There are many different types of symbols used to refer to the space groups and the various settings. The most well-known are:
    • Hermann–Mauguin (HM) symbols: of which there is a "short", "full", and "extended" variety.
    • Hall symbols.
  • Specifically a set of 530 "conventional spacegroup settings" has appeared primarily based on what is shown in the IT tables. However, of these are actually only 527 symmetrically unique, three pairs of conventional settings, all in spacegroup #68, are equivalent (and, hence, share the same Hall symbol):
    • C_c_c_a:1 (#68:1) and C_c_c_b:1 (#68:1ba-c) -- Hall symbol: C 2 2 -1ac
    • A_b_a_a:1 (#68:1cab) and A_c_a_a:1 (#68:1-cba) -- Hall symbol: A 2 2 -1ab
    • B_b_c_b:1 (#68:1bca) and B_b_a_b: (#68:1a-cb) -- Hall symbol: B 2 2 -1ab
  • Some IT standard HM symbols were changed in the Fourth Edition of the IT tables (IT 1995) to replace the glide plane symbol: "For the five space groups Aem2 (39), Aea2 (41), Cmce (64), Cmme (67) and Ccce (68), the ‘new’ space-group symbols, containing the symbol ‘e’ for the ‘double’ glide plane, are given for all settings. These symbols were first introduced in the Fourth Edition of this volume (IT 1995); cf. Foreword to the Fourth Edition. For further explanations, see Section 1.3.2, Note (x) and the space-group diagrams".
  • Some Hall symbols differ between the IT 2001 and forward edition, and earlier lists, with one of the primary changes being dropping the lattice symbol "S".

  • Of the 230 space group types, there are 11 pairs that only differ by chirality: these are:
    • P 41 - (#76) and P 43 - (#78)
    • P 41 2 2 - (#91) and P 43 2 2 - (#95)
    • P 41 21 2 - (#92) and P 43 21 2 - (#96)
    • P 31 - (#144) and P 32 - (#145)
    • P 31 1 2 - (#151) and P 32 1 2 - (#153)

    • P 62 - (#171) and P 64 - (#172)
